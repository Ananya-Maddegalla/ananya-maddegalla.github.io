I have 5 years of experience in battery electrochemistry, specializing in DOE, testing, and analyzing cell performance for Magnesium anode, cathode, and non-aqueous Mg electrolyte chemistries for Rechargeable Mg batteries. Additionally, I have experience in Li-ion and Na-ion batteries beyond my Ph.D. thesis work. I am an expert in pre- and post-physical, chemical data characterization, electrode fabrication, and validation of material performance for coin cell and 3-electrode cell systems. Research

During the course of my PhD, my research work focused on developing and designing of novel electrodes (anode and cathode) and electrolyte formulation in rechargeable magneisum-ion batteries (RMBs). Currently I'm working on desigining High-Entropy cathode materials for Na-ion Batteries and improving electrochemical performance of NMC cathode for Li-ion Batteries.

I have worked on evaluating the performance of ultra thin AZ31 alloy as a replacement for Mg metal anode in RMBs. I was leading this project analysing the anode-electroltyte interface using Chronoamperometry technique in Al-Phenyl Complex (APC) Mg electrolyte solution and evaluating the surface of AZ31 alloy anode using HR-SEM and XRF spectroscopy. To evaluate the performance of ultrathin Az31 alloy, I performed full cell studies such as, galvanostatic cycling and rate performance studies in protytpe Mg coin cell using Chevrel Phase Cathodes. This work is an important milestone for practical application of ultrathin Mg alloys in RMBs. This project was a framework of E-Magic FET-OPEN Consortia in the funding frame of Horizon 2020 Program (Grant Agreement No. 824066), and from the Israeli Ministry of Science and Technology (Israel-Germany batteries school program). I did this work in collaboration with Alberto Blazquez, CIDETEC, Basque Research and Technology Alliance (BRTA), Spain, Dr. Jean Frédéric Martin, Université Grenoble Alpes, Grenoble, France, and Prof. Y. Ein-Eli, Technion-Israel Institute of Technology, and Prof. Doron Aurbach at BIU.

For my next project I worked on understanding the role of electroactive species i.e. anionic and cationic complexes in ethereal Mg eletrolyte solutions. For this project I did collobration with Prof. Yitzak Apeloig and his research group at Technion-Israel Intitute of Technology, working closing with Senior Reseacher Dr. Dmitry Bravo-Zhivotovskii, who is an expert in Shlenck synthesis of air-senstive non-aqueous organometallic complex syntheis. We synthesised and isolated anion selective and cation selective electroactive species for Mg electrolyte solutions using Grignard and Non-grignard based precursors and Al based Lewis acid. We investigated their structure with SC-XRD, Raman and multi-NMR Spectroscopy technqiues. For the Electrochemcial analysis of the novel electrolyte solution, we perfromed CV, LSV, Glavnostatic CP, and we examined its full cellcycling performance with Mg protoype coin cell using Galvanostatic cylcing and Rate performance techniques. The synthesis and understanding of this work can lead to new approaches for a rational design of ionic species and development of more refined and optimized electrolyte solutions with wide electrochemical window and allowing compatibitlity with high-energy density cathode materials for RMB.

In the future I would like to dive deeper into the theoretical aspects of cryptography and secure multiparty computation and study other cryptographic primitives.
